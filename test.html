Problem: Days to Relax

You are given a list of daily productivity scores for a certain task. The productivity score of each day is represented by an integer. You need to determine for each day how many days you would have to wait until a higher productivity score is recorded. If there isn't a future day with a higher score, the result for that day should be 0.

Input:

An array scores where scores[i] is the productivity score recorded on the ith day.

Output:

Return an array result such that result[i] is the number of days you would have to wait after the ith day to get a higher score. If there is no future day with a higher score, set result[i] = 0.

Example:

    Input:
    scores = [50, 55, 53, 60, 58, 70, 61]
    
    Output:
    [1, 3, 2, 0, 1, 0, 0]

Explanation:
- On day 1, you must wait 1 day for a higher score (55).
- On day 2, you must wait 3 days for a higher score (60).
- On day 3, you must wait 2 days for a higher score (60).
- On day 4, you do not need to wait any days as the next day will have a lower score.
- On day 5, you must wait 1 day for a higher score (70).
- On day 6, you do not need to wait any days as there are no more days with a higher score.
- On day 7, you do not need to wait any days as there are no more days with a higher score.

Constraints:

  • 1 ≤ length of scores ≤ 100,000
  • 30 ≤ scores[i] ≤ 100
